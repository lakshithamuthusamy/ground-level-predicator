Problem Statement – Ground Water Level Predictor

Groundwater is one of the most important sources of fresh water for agriculture, industries, and domestic use. Due to increasing population, urbanization, climate change, and excessive groundwater extraction, groundwater levels are declining in many regions. Predicting groundwater levels using traditional methods is time-consuming and often inaccurate.

The Ground Water Level Predictor aims to solve this problem by using Machine Learning to predict future groundwater levels based on factors such as rainfall, temperature, and previous groundwater levels. This helps farmers, researchers, and government agencies make informed decisions about water management, conservation, and sustainable groundwater usage.

Objective:

To develop a web-based application that predicts groundwater levels accurately using historical environmental data, enabling better planning and efficient water resource management.

Solution 

The Ground Water Level Predictor provides an intelligent solution by using Machine Learning to estimate future groundwater levels from historical environmental data. The application analyzes key factors such as rainfall, temperature, and previous groundwater levels to generate accurate predictions.

The system offers a simple web interface where users can enter input values and instantly receive the predicted groundwater level. This enables farmers, researchers, and government organizations to make informed decisions about water usage, irrigation planning, and groundwater conservation.

Key Features

1.Predicts groundwater levels using a trained Machine Learning model.
2.Accepts inputs such as rainfall, temperature, and previous groundwater level.
3.Provides quick and accurate predictions through a user-friendly web application.
4.Displays prediction results in a clear and easy-to-understand format.
5.Can be extended with real-time weather and groundwater data for improved accuracy.

Benefits

1.Supports sustainable groundwater management.
2.Helps farmers plan irrigation efficiently.
3.Assists government agencies and researchers in monitoring groundwater resources.
4.Reduces the risk of groundwater depletion through data-driven decision-making.
5.Promotes effective water conservation and resource planning.
